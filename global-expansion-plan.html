Every Blocker. Destroyed.

Here’s every objection you’ll hear about why this can’t work internationally — and why each one is wrong.

BlockerRealityPrecedent
❌ “You need local Power of Attorney” No. We’re a form preparation service, not a legal representative. The client is the applicant. We prepare answers; they submit. TurboTax, H&R Block, Fightback4Justice
❌ “Cross-border data protection” Explicit recorded consent + DPIA. UK has EU adequacy. Australia allows with consent. Canada allows with contractual safeguards. Every international SaaS company
❌ “You need a licence” Benefits assistance is unregulated in UK, Australia, and Canada. USA: “form preparation” is not regulated (same as TurboTax). TurboTax operates without CPA licence
❌ “Portals need local ID” Client uses their own credentials (MyGov, GCKey, Login.gov). We never touch their identity — we prepare answers, they authenticate. H&R Block online tax filing
❌ “Fee caps in the USA” The 25%/$9,200 cap applies to SSA-registered representatives. Flat fee for form preparation avoids it entirely. TurboTax charges flat fees, not %
❌ “Non-English forms” Interview is in English. Form fields are data-mapped once per benefit type. The browser fills known selectors — it doesn’t need to read. All international form automation
❌ “Government portals block foreign IPs” They don’t. Government portals are designed for citizens abroad. If needed: cloud instances in target country. Portals explicitly support overseas access
